About

I am a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence (AI), remote sensing, and precision agriculture. I hold a B.Sc. in Agricultural Engineering (2016) from the University of Seville (US), where I received an award for distinguished academic performance, and two master’s degrees (2017) with extraordinary end-of-studies distinctions. My research began with an internship at IFAPA, where I published my first peer-reviewed paper. In 2018, I secured a PIF Fellowship from the US to pursue my Ph.D. (2022), focusing on AI, drones, and remote sensing for precision agriculture, with a six-month research stay at Wageningen University & Research (Netherlands) on drone-based yield estimation.

My postdoctoral work includes positions at Ghent University, researching hyperspectral imaging and AI for crop monitoring and disease detection, Hasselt University & Flanders AI Academy in data science applications, and currently at KU Leuven, working on generative adversarial networks (GANs) and satellite imagery to assess vegetation changes under climate change.

With over 264 hours of official university instruction and 167 hours of lifelong learning courses, I have published 12 peer-reviewed papers, 4 outreach articles, presented at 10+ international conferences, and authored a book and a book chapter. Additionally, I have supervised 6 MSc, 5 BSc, and 1 PhD thesis. My research has received over 318 citations (h-index: 8, Scopus) and 466 citations (h-index: 9, Google Scholar). I am passionate about AI-driven solutions for sustainable agriculture and environmental monitoring.
